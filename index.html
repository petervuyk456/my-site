Personal Philosophies

  1. I am firmly beholden to the notion that if you are going to do something (and I mean anything), you do it well AT MINIMUM.
    At best, you astound.
  2. Constructive feedback is the fuel to my engine. I will never take it personally, just give it to me straight.
  3. I love to learn new things proportional to their level of difficulty and overall utility.
  4. When I say "I aim to please", I really mean "I aim to deliver solutions that are well-tested, user-centric, modern, and bad-ass".
    This mentality tends to leave my customers satisfied, but the main reason for the approach is that it's a hell of an effective way to learn and achieve mastery.
  5. I adhere to the philosophy that the mind directs the body, but that you are not your mind. In other words, Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs for personal development.
    Challenging yourself physically (eating well, hard exercise, good hygiene, etc.) establishes a foundation upon which we can truly push ourselves mentally (knowledge, memorization/learning rate, intuition, mindfulness etc.) establishes a foundation upon which we can truly change and improve our whole self through self-reflection and self-actualization.
  6. I appreciate a dynamic and evolving workflow/workload (it expands the mind and, in moderation, improves our ability to manage time and ideas) but directed focus is where I thrive.
  7. Almost every great idea ever had has been recorded, discussed, and contradicted in a book/blog/podcast. And that is the story of why you should read and listen before forming opinions.

Enjoy exploring my site, and feel free to reach out with questions, project ideas, or work.
